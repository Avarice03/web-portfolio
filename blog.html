Offlane

Life of an Offlaner

What is an offlaner?

Offlaner is one of the roles in the online game Dota 2. Dota 2 or Defence Of The Ancients 2 is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA). It is a game played between two teams with five players each. The match is played by 10 persons which controls their individual heroes with different roles. Generally, these roles in Dota 2 are classified in 5 positions to make a good team composition. These positions are:


  • Carry (Position 1)
  • Mid (Position 2)
  • Offlane (Position 3)
  • Soft-Support (Position 4)
  • Hard-Support (Position 5)

The offlane role

The offlane role or position 3 is usually described among these names: the hard lane, the suicide lane, the masochist lane. As an offlaner, your heroes usually should be tanky, durable, and can set important plays. It is one of the hardest roles in Dota since you usually take up most of the damage from the enemies and should still manage to come up on top (or at least be the reason to win a clash). Playing the offlane role can really be frustrating since you need to create space for your team and endure the lane even if your support (position 4) leaves and helps the other lanes. This means that you will usually be in a 1v2 or even 1v3 situations where 2 or more enemies are trying to kill you. But even with these challenges, an offlaner should have a lot of patience and try to have a clear mindset to win the game. That's why personally, I call the offlane as a masochist lane.

Goals of an offlaner

As an offlaner, your main goals should be:

  1. Initiate and set up team fights
  2. Synergize with the other roles
  3. Soak up damage for the team
  4. Know your timing when and not to fight
  5. Create space for the team
  6. Build items that will help the survivability of the team
  7. Think positive all throughout the game
  8. Delay the items of the enemy carry
  9. Plan and secure game objectives
  10. Denying creeps in the lane
  11. Utilize wards in the lane
  12. Fight to secure objectives
  13. Prevent being underleveled
  14. Stop enemy pulling
  15. Pick the appropriate heroes for the offlane role (e.g. tanks, team fight iniators, high pushing potential)

Me as an offlaner

You probably know as of now, I'm playing Dota 2 as an offlaner. I admit that there are so many times I get frustrated while playing because of the hardships of an offlaner. But still, it is very fun since you will feel hyped for some moments especially when you win a clash because of your godly sets. I'm having fun playing as an offlaner since you will get a lot of positive feedback when you initiate and win a team fight. However, there will also be a lot of pressure for you since the outcome of a team fight you initiated depends on your actions. The most annoying sensation for me is when I'm facing 2 enemies while being alone since my support went to gank other lanes. As my teammate have said "my purpose is to tank for my pos 4 to shine" even if it should have been the other way around. It is annoying since even if I'm having a hard time, I should endure and take it as a grown man since I'm also aware that these hardships comes with the role as an offlane. If you will decide to play the offlane role after reading this, stick with it and enjoy the experience as you improve as an offlaner.

My tips as an offlaner

  1. Be aware of the environment and always check the minimap
  2. Always try to iniate fights in high ground to secure advantage
  3. Use in and out method in team clashes
  4. Communicate clearly with your other teammates before going into fights
  5. Optimize your game settings for the best experience
  6. Know when to blink in and when not to not
  7. Know the potential of your enemies and adjust to it
  8. Secure the creep equilibrium in the lane
  9. Trade hits with the other enemies constantly
  10. Focus on getting exp rather than gold
  11. Try and secure a kill once you hit level 6 in the lane
  12. Commend your position 4 for doing great every game
  13. Try not to get tilted when your teammate decides to throw (Milagro)
  14. Practice every game the offlane role
  15. Love your teammates and the game
